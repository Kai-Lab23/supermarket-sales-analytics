3. Sales and profit distribution across product lines and cities are relatively balanced, indicating that the business is not highly dependent on a single product category or city.
4. Daily sales performance is relatively stable, with no major difference between days.
5. Hourly sales patterns show higher activity around lunch and evening hours, indicating potential customer behavior patterns during break times.

Business Recommendations

1. Focus on increasing transaction volume because profit margin is relatively constant.
2. Maintain balanced sales performance across product categories and cities to reduce dependency on a single segment.
3. Optimize stock and staff readiness during peak activity hours, especially around lunch and evening periods.
4. Monitor lower-performing hours to identify limited opportunities for targeted promotions.
5. Interpret correlation results carefully, especially for variables that are directly related by calculation.
